Co-op Work Term 1

University of Guelph, Research Assistant - Food Science

Marangoni in the Food Science. This website will go over the details of my time at this job, the employer, and my overall experience.

Job Description

The project I worked on during this term was a Triglyceride Property Calculator which is used to estimate the melting points and enthalpies of triacylglycerides (TAGs). The app was originally developed in R back in 2017 by another team and left untouched since then. In this term it was my job to optimize the version of the app in R and also translate it to Python. After the optimization and translation of the app into the new language, I focused my work on implementing additional features to the existing Python version. Making the app more feasible to users, creating a more accurate computation, and updating the database were my main goals.
The most interesting part of this job was applying myself into a completely different field which I had very little knowledge in and tying it back into my computing background. Learning the theory behind the app and then writing software as the answer was a very unique and satisfying feeling that I didn't know this career could bring me. The base skills I needed for this job was past full stack development and a base understanding of systems which I learned both in classes and on my own time but my understanding and confidence grew considerably by everyday practice on the job.

Conclusions

My time contributing to the Triglyceride Property Calculator was a very new and fun experience. I created many amazing memories with my supervisor and colleagues. I learned a substantial amount and it is knowledge that I will carry with me throughout my career. This being my first job in this industry, the experience was incredible and I plan to keep in touch with the amazing people in the Food Science department at the University of Guelph.
